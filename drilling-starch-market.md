<p><p>The drilling starch market consists of two primary types: high-temperature starch and low-temperature starch. High-temperature starch is designed for applications in challenging drilling environments, providing enhanced stability and viscosity at elevated temperatures. Conversely, low-temperature starch is suited for use in cooler drilling conditions, offering effective fluid loss control and improved performance in less extreme settings. Both types play crucial roles in ensuring efficient drilling operations, enhancing mud properties, and optimizing wellbore stability.</p></p>
