<p><p>The orthopedic screwdriver market is divided into two main types: electric and manual. Electric orthopedic screwdrivers are powered tools designed for precision and efficiency, allowing for quicker insertion of screws during surgical procedures, reducing fatigue for surgeons. In contrast, manual orthopedic screwdrivers are handheld tools providing greater control and tactile feedback, often preferred in delicate or complex surgeries. The choice between electric and manual options depends on the specific surgical requirements, surgeon preference, and the complexity of the procedure.</p></p>
