<p><p>The Residual Solvent Analysis Instrument market primarily features two types: Gas Chromatography (GC) and Gas Chromatography Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS). GC is utilized for separating volatile compounds in a sample, providing a profile of residual solvents. In contrast, GC-MS combines chromatographic separation with mass spectrometric detection, enabling precise identification and quantification of compounds at low concentrations. Together, these technologies ensure compliance with safety regulations and quality control in pharmaceuticals, food, and chemical industries, enhancing product safety and efficacy.</p></p>
