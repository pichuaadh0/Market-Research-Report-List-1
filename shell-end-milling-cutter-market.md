<p><p>The shell-end milling cutter market comprises three main types: carbide, steel, and ceramic. Carbide cutters are known for their hardness and wear resistance, making them ideal for high-speed applications. Steel cutters are more economical and suitable for general-purpose milling but may wear faster than carbide. Ceramic cutters offer superior heat resistance and are ideal for high-speed machining of hard materials, though they can be more brittle. Each type caters to specific machining needs and production environments.</p></p>
