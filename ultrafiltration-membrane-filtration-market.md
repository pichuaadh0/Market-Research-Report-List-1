<p><p>The ultrafiltration membrane filtration market is categorized into two main types: inorganic membranes and organic membranes. Inorganic membranes, typically made from materials such as ceramics or metals, offer high thermal and chemical stability, making them suitable for harsh environments and specific applications. On the other hand, organic membranes, usually composed of polymers, are more versatile and widely used for water treatment and food processing due to their cost-effectiveness and selective permeability. Each type addresses distinct filtration needs based on application requirements.</p></p>
